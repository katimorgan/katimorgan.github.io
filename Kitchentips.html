  • Prep before you cook
    Chop everything first, measure ingredients, then start cooking. It keeps things calm instead of chaotic.

  • Don’t overcrowd the pan
    If food is packed too tightly, it steams instead of browning. Give it space for better flavor.
  • Salt in layers
    Add a little salt as you cook, not just at the end. Food tastes more balanced this way.
  • Butter + anything = better
    If your food tastes “meh,” add a little butter + salt. That’s basically restaurant logic
  • High heat ≠ better
    Medium heat is your best friend 90% of the time.
